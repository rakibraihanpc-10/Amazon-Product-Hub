What to Look for in a Glue Stick

Honestly, the best glue stick for kids should be easy to use, non-toxic, and mess-free. It's also a good idea to look for a glue stick that dries quickly, as this will help prevent accidents and frustration.

I also recommend choosing a glue stick that's specifically designed for kids. These often come with safety features like tamper-evident caps and are formulated to be gentler on surfaces.

Using Your Pritt Glue Stick

To get the most out of your Pritt glue stick, make sure to use it on the right surfaces. This glue stick works beautifully on paper, cardboard, and fabric, but it may not be the best choice for heavier materials like wood or metal.

Truth be told, the key to using this glue stick effectively is to apply a small amount at a time. This will help prevent the glue from oozing out too quickly and making a mess.

Also, be sure to clean up any excess glue immediately. This will prevent it from drying on the surface and making a sticky mess.
