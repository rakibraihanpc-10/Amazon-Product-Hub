Key Features to Look For

In addition to sound quality, there are a few other key features to consider. First, think about the type of noise-cancelling technology used. Some headphones use active noise control, which actively emits a "negative" sound wave to cancel out background noise. Others use passive noise isolation, which uses physical materials to block out sound. I've found that active noise control tends to be more effective, but it can also be more expensive. Honestly, it's worth the investment - the difference is night and day.

Another thing to consider is battery life. If you're planning to use your headphones on long flights or commutes, you'll want to look for ones with long battery life.
